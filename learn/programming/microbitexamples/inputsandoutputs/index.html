1.2 Pins


micro:bit pins are ways for you to connect different electronics to the board. The micro:bit has 3 pins that you can access normally, but there are things you can buy called breakout boards that give you access to more pins. The holes in the photo below labeled 0, 1, and 2 are the pins. To the right of them you can see two more holes labeled 3V and GND. These are, respectively, the power and ground pins.

Pins 0, 1, 2 are data pins -- meaning that they can be used to read data (e.g. you want to read the moisture levels from a humidity sensor) and to write data (e.g. you want to tell a connected motor how fast to spin).
